BENEFITS OF AMOXICILLIN

Precautionary Measures of Amoxicillin

Check for allergies – Do not take if allergic to penicillin or cephalosporins.

Follow doctor’s prescription – Take the exact dose and complete the full course.

Watch for side effects – Report rashes, difficulty breathing, or severe diarrhea immediately.

Avoid self-medicating – Never share antibiotics with others.

Be cautious with other medicines – Inform your doctor if you are taking blood thinners or birth control pills.

Storage – Keep in a cool, dry place and away from children (refrigerate liquid form if required).

Possible Effects of Amoxicillin

Amoxicillin may have an effect when using it. Some of it is:

– Nausea
– Vomiting
– Diarrhea
– Temporary discoloration (yellow, brown, or gray color) of your teeth

Serious Effects of Amoxicillin

– Rash
– Skin blisters or peeling
– Itching
– Hives
– Wheezing
– Difficulty swallowing or breathing
– Swelling of the face, throat, tongue, lips, and eyes
– Prolonged vomiting, diarrhea, extreme tiredness, and paleness that may occur within 24 hours
– Severe diarrhea (watery or bloody stools) that may occur with or without fever and stomach cramps (may occur up to 2 months after treatment)

If you experience any of these symptoms, call your doctor immediately or stop taking amoxicillin.

How to Dispose of Medicines at Home

When a take-back option is not easily available, there are two ways to dispose of medicines at home, depending on the drug.

Flushing Medicines

Some medicines could be especially harmful to others, and they have specific directions to immediately flush them down the sink or toilet when no longer needed. Check the label or patient information leaflet. Don’t flush unless it is on the official flush list.

Disposing in Household Trash

Almost all medicines, except those on the FDA flush list, can be thrown into household trash: pills, liquids, drops, patches, and creams.

Steps:
– Remove drugs from containers and mix with coffee grounds, dirt, or cat litter.
– Place mixture in a sealed bag or container.
– Throw in household trash.
– Scratch out personal information on medicine packaging.
